About the project

IDEA

Japan is often perceived as a nation with a strong sense of cultural identity, proud and protective of its traditions

While this statement holds true in many respects, this project aims not to challenge this perception but to provide a deeper understanding of the country's multifaceted identity.

There's a fascinating blend of traditional values and Western influences in Japan, particularly noticeable in what some call Western Japan.

This influence touches every aspect of society, including one of its most iconic cultural representations being the Mangas.

Knowledge Organization

THEORETICAL MODEL

The ultimate outcome of our domain-specific data modeling efforts is represented by the Theoretical Model. This model comprehensively describes, using natural language, all identified properties of each item and the connections among the entities in our domain. Additional entities were introduced at this phase to enhance the data with an interpretative layer.

The model provides insights into WHO is involved for human-related aspects, WHERE for geographical contexts, WHEN for temporal contexts, and WHAT for thematic or conceptual elements concerning each object.

In the bottom-right corner of the model, "Keys" illustrate the visual distinctions used to denote the parameters that respond to each of these queries.


Knowledge Organization

ENHANCED E/R MODEL

The Theoretical Model, initially crafted using natural language, is now articulated through the Entity/Relationship framework into an enhanced iteration of the initial E/R Model. This updated diagram systematically portrays individuals, locations, timelines, and types as Entities, highlighting their interrelations as Relationships.

Knowledge Organization

CONCEPTUAL MODEL

In the concluding phase, the project advances from the Theoretical Model to a more structured Conceptual Model. This shift employs an ontological framework to represent our domain while maintaining consistency in our workflow. The entities, themes, and connections initially identified in the Theoretical Model are systematically reorganized into classes and properties derived from carefully chosen schemas, vocabularies, and ontologies. Additionally, to clarify ambiguities and enhance the accuracy, interoperability, and practicality of our linked data, our team has aligned significant entities with authoritative resources.
